Chicken Tikka Masala

Roasted marinated chicken chunks in a spiced, creamy curry sauce.

Ingredients

  • 1.5 lbs Boneless Chicken Thighs
  • 1 cup Plain Yogurt
  • 2 tbsp Garam Masala
  • 1 can (14oz) Tomato Puree
  • 1/2 cup Heavy Cream
  • 3 Cloves Garlic & 1 tbsp Ginger, minced

Instructions

  1. Mix yogurt, half the ginger/garlic, and 1 tbsp garam masala. Marinate chicken for at least 1 hour.
  2. Broil or grill the marinated chicken until slightly charred and cooked through. Set aside.
  3. In a large pot, sauté the remaining ginger and garlic. Add tomato puree and remaining spices, simmering for 15 minutes.
  4. Stir in the heavy cream and let the sauce thicken slightly over low heat.
  5. Add the cooked chicken pieces into the sauce, simmer for 5 more minutes, and serve hot with rice or naan.

Palak Paneer

A classic North Indian dish featuring fresh spinach puree and cubes of mild paneer cheese.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb Fresh Spinach
  • 10 oz Paneer (Indian Cottage Cheese), cubed
  • 1 Medium Onion, finely chopped
  • 1 tsp Cumin Seeds
  • 1/4 cup Heavy Cream

Instructions

  1. Blanch the spinach in boiling water for 2 minutes, immediately transfer to an ice bath, and then blend into a smooth puree.
  2. Lightly pan-fry the paneer cubes in oil until the edges are golden brown. Remove and set aside.
  3. In the same pan, temper the cumin seeds until fragrant, then sauté the onions until golden.
  4. Add the spinach puree and simmer for 5-7 minutes.
  5. Stir in the cream, gently fold in the paneer cubes, and simmer for 2 more minutes before serving.

Suggested Next Steps

Swap Paneer → Extra Firm Tofu

Confidence: High

Press extra-firm tofu to remove moisture, cube it, and pan-fry it exactly as you would the paneer.

Why? Tofu has an incredibly similar texture and mild flavor profile to paneer, making it a seamless visual and textural substitute.

Naturally Gluten-Free

Confidence: High

No swaps needed. Palak Paneer does not contain any gluten ingredients.

Why? The thickness of the dish comes entirely from the pureed spinach and the fat content of the dairy/cream.

Chana Masala

A hearty, tangy, and slightly spicy chickpea curry bursting with flavor.

Ingredients

  • 2 cans (15oz) Chickpeas, rinsed
  • 1 large Onion, finely chopped
  • 2 Tomatoes, diced
  • 1 tbsp Chana Masala spice blend
  • Fresh Cilantro for garnish

Instructions

  1. Heat oil in a heavy-bottomed pot and sauté the onions until deeply caramelized and brown.
  2. Add the tomatoes and cook until they break down entirely into a thick paste.
  3. Stir in the Chana Masala spice blend and cook for 1 minute to bloom the spices.
  4. Add the chickpeas and 1 cup of water. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 20 minutes.
  5. Lightly mash a few chickpeas with the back of your spoon to naturally thicken the gravy. Garnish with cilantro.

Customize Recipe

Suggested Next Steps

Use Cooking Oil instead of Ghee

Confidence: High

This recipe is already 100% plant-based, just make sure to use a neutral vegetable oil for sautéing.

Why? Some traditional Indian restaurants use Ghee (clarified butter) to cook the onions, but oil is a perfectly standard alternative.

Check Pre-made Spice Blends

Confidence: Med

While the ingredients are gluten-free, always check the label of store-bought Chana Masala spice blends.

Why? Some commercial spice mixes use wheat flour as an anti-caking agent to keep the spices from clumping in the box.

Vegetable Biryani

An aromatic, deeply flavorful mixed rice dish layered with spiced vegetables.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups Basmati Rice, washed and soaked
  • 2 cups Mixed Veggies (Carrots, Peas, Cauliflower)
  • 1/2 cup Plain Yogurt
  • 2 tbsp Ghee (Clarified Butter)
  • Biryani whole spices (Cinnamon, Cardamom, Cloves)

Instructions

  1. Parboil the soaked Basmati rice with whole spices until 70% cooked. Drain and set aside.
  2. Marinate the mixed vegetables in yogurt and powdered spices (turmeric, chili powder, biryani masala) for 30 minutes.
  3. In a heavy-bottomed pot, cook the marinated vegetables in ghee until tender.
  4. Layer the partially cooked rice evenly over the vegetables. Add a splash of saffron milk on top.
  5. Cover the pot tightly and cook on the lowest possible heat for 15-20 minutes to steam and finish cooking the rice (Dum method).

Customize Recipe

Suggested Next Steps

Swap Ghee/Yogurt → Coconut Oil & Dairy-free Yogurt

Confidence: High

Use coconut oil instead of ghee, and marinate the vegetables in a plain, unsweetened almond or soy yogurt.

Why? The yogurt is needed to tenderize the vegetables and create a thick base gravy, which plant-based yogurts achieve perfectly.

Naturally Gluten-Free

Confidence: High

Rice and vegetables are the stars here, making this dish entirely free of gluten.

Why? Authentic Biryani relies entirely on long-grain Basmati rice, never wheat or noodles.

Murgh Makhani (Butter Chicken)

A mild, sweet, and incredibly rich tomato-based curry cooked with butter and cream.

Ingredients

  • 1.5 lbs Boneless Chicken Breasts
  • 1/4 cup Butter
  • 1 large Onion, blended to a paste
  • 1.5 cups Tomato Puree
  • 1/2 cup Heavy Cream
  • 1 tbsp Kasuri Methi (Dried Fenugreek Leaves)

Instructions

  1. Briefly pan-fry the chicken breast pieces until sealed, then remove from the pan.
  2. Melt the butter in the same pan. Sauté the onion paste until it loses its raw smell.
  3. Pour in the tomato puree, add your spices (coriander, cumin, mild chili), and simmer for 15 minutes until the oil separates from the sauce.
  4. Crush the Kasuri Methi between your palms and sprinkle it into the sauce.
  5. Return the chicken to the pan, stir in the heavy cream, and simmer for 10 minutes until the chicken is fully cooked.

Suggested Next Steps

Swap Chicken & Dairy → Cauliflower & Cashew Cream

Confidence: High

Roast cauliflower florets instead of chicken, cook with vegan butter, and finish the sauce with a thick cashew cream.

Why? The magic of Butter Chicken is the mild, buttery tomato gravy. Cashew cream provides the same luxurious mouthfeel as heavy dairy cream without breaking.

Naturally Gluten-Free

Confidence: High

This rich curry is naturally gluten-free. Just skip the naan!

Why? The sauce gets its silky, thick texture from the pureed onions and heavy cream, requiring no flour thickeners.
