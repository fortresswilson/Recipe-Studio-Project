Tacos al Pastor

Thinly sliced, marinated pork roasted with pineapple and served on warm tortillas.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs Pork Shoulder, thinly sliced
  • 3 Guajillo Chiles & 2 Ancho Chiles, rehydrated
  • 1/2 cup Pineapple Juice & 1 cup Pineapple Chunks
  • 2 tbsp Achiote Paste
  • 10 Small Corn or Flour Tortillas
  • Chopped Onion & Cilantro for garnish

Instructions

  1. Blend the rehydrated chiles, pineapple juice, achiote paste, and a pinch of cumin to create the marinade.
  2. Coat the sliced pork in the marinade and refrigerate for at least 4 hours.
  3. In a very hot skillet (or on a grill), cook the marinated pork and pineapple chunks until the edges are slightly charred.
  4. Chop the cooked pork into bite-sized pieces.
  5. Warm the tortillas and assemble the tacos, garnishing with fresh onion, cilantro, and a squeeze of lime.

Why? Authentic Mexican street tacos exclusively use corn tortillas, which are naturally gluten-free and offer a superior flavor.

Authentic Guacamole

A fresh, chunky dip made from ripe avocados, lime juice, and aromatics.

Ingredients

  • 3 Ripe Haas Avocados
  • 1/2 small White Onion, finely diced
  • 1 Jalapeño, seeded and minced
  • 1/4 cup Fresh Cilantro, chopped
  • 1-2 Limes, juiced

Instructions

  1. Halve the avocados, remove the pits, and scoop the flesh into a large bowl.
  2. Using a fork or potato masher, gently mash the avocados. Leave it slightly chunky for better texture.
  3. Fold in the diced onion, minced jalapeño, and chopped cilantro.
  4. Pour the fresh lime juice over the mixture and season generously with salt.
  5. Stir to combine. Taste and adjust salt or lime juice as needed. Serve immediately with tortilla chips.

Naturally Vegan

Confidence: High

This recipe is entirely plant-based. No modifications are needed!

Why? Authentic guacamole relies strictly on fresh produce. Keep dairy out of it!

Check Your Tortilla Chips

Confidence: High

The guacamole is gluten-free, but be careful with the chips you dip into it.

Why? While corn chips are naturally gluten-free, restaurant or store-bought chips are often fried in the same oil as wheat products, leading to cross-contamination.

Red Chile Chicken Enchiladas

Corn tortillas rolled around shredded chicken, baked in a rich red chile sauce and topped with melted cheese.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups Cooked Chicken, shredded
  • 12 Corn Tortillas
  • 2 cups Red Enchilada Sauce
  • 2 cups Monterey Jack Cheese, shredded
  • 1/4 cup Neutral Oil (for frying)

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F. Spread a thin layer of enchilada sauce in the bottom of a 9x13 baking dish.
  2. Briefly fry each corn tortilla in hot oil for about 5 seconds per side to soften them and prevent cracking. Drain on paper towels.
  3. Dip a softened tortilla into the enchilada sauce, fill with shredded chicken and a pinch of cheese, roll tightly, and place seam-side down in the baking dish.
  4. Pour the remaining enchilada sauce evenly over the rolled tortillas and top with the rest of the shredded cheese.
  5. Bake for 20 minutes until the cheese is bubbling and slightly golden.

Classic Chiles Rellenos

Roasted poblano peppers stuffed with melting cheese, coated in a fluffy egg batter, and fried.

Ingredients

  • 4 Large Poblano Peppers
  • 1.5 cups Oaxaca Cheese, cut into sticks
  • 3 Large Eggs, separated
  • 1/4 cup All-Purpose Flour (for dusting)
  • Oil for frying

Instructions

  1. Roast the poblanos over an open flame or under a broiler until the skin is blistered and black. Place in a covered bowl to steam for 10 minutes, then peel off the skin.
  2. Carefully slit the side of each pepper, remove the seeds, and stuff with the sticks of Oaxaca cheese. Dust the stuffed peppers lightly with flour.
  3. Beat the egg whites until stiff peaks form. Gently fold in the egg yolks to create a fluffy batter.
  4. Heat 1 inch of oil in a skillet. Dip each stuffed pepper into the fluffy egg batter, ensuring it's fully coated.
  5. Fry the peppers, turning once, until golden brown on all sides. Serve immediately, often topped with a light tomato salsa.

Suggested Next Steps

Bake with a Quinoa Stuffing

Confidence: High

Skip the egg batter entirely. Stuff the roasted peppers with a spiced quinoa, corn, and black bean mixture, then bake them.

Why? The traditional fluffy egg batter is incredibly difficult to replicate without eggs. A baked, hearty stuffed pepper is a cleaner, easier, and delicious plant-based alternative.

Swap Flour Dusting → Cornstarch

Confidence: High

Dust the stuffed poblano peppers with cornstarch or a gluten-free all-purpose flour blend instead of wheat flour.

Why? The dusting step is crucial because it gives the fluffy egg batter a dry surface to cling to during frying. Cornstarch works perfectly for this.

Sizzling Beef Fajitas

Marinated skirt steak pan-seared with bell peppers and onions.

Ingredients

  • 1.5 lbs Skirt Steak
  • 1 Red and 1 Green Bell Pepper, sliced
  • 1 Large White Onion, sliced
  • 1/4 cup Lime Juice & 2 tbsp Soy Sauce
  • 1 tbsp Cumin & 1 tbsp Chili Powder

Instructions

  1. Whisk the lime juice, soy sauce, cumin, chili powder, and a splash of oil. Marinate the skirt steak in this mixture for 2-4 hours.
  2. Heat a large cast-iron skillet until smoking hot. Remove the steak from the marinade and sear for 3-4 minutes per side. Remove and let rest.
  3. In the same hot skillet, add the sliced bell peppers and onions. Cook until charred on the edges but still crisp-tender.
  4. Slice the rested steak thinly against the grain.
  5. Return the sliced steak to the skillet with the vegetables, toss to combine, and serve sizzling with warm tortillas.

Suggested Next Steps

Swap Beef → Portobello Mushrooms

Confidence: High

Slice large portobello mushroom caps into thick strips and marinate them just like you would the steak.

Why? Portobello mushrooms have a rich, earthy, meaty texture and act like sponges, soaking up the savory lime and soy marinade rapidly.

Swap Soy Sauce → Tamari

Confidence: High

Use Tamari or coconut aminos in your marinade instead of traditional soy sauce.

Why? Standard soy sauce is brewed with roasted wheat. Tamari offers the exact same umami flavor profile but is traditionally brewed without gluten.
