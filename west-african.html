Classic Jollof Rice

A vibrant, one-pot rice dish cooked in a rich, spicy tomato and red bell pepper stew.

Ingredients

  • 3 cups Long-grain Parboiled Rice
  • 4 Roma Tomatoes & 2 Red Bell Peppers
  • 1 Scotch Bonnet Pepper & 2 Onions
  • 1/2 cup Tomato Paste
  • 3 cups Chicken Broth
  • 1 tbsp Thyme & 2 Bay Leaves

Instructions

  1. Blend the tomatoes, red bell peppers, scotch bonnet, and one onion into a smooth puree. Boil the puree down in a pot until the water evaporates.
  2. In a large, heavy-bottomed pot, fry diced onions in vegetable oil. Add tomato paste and fry for 5 minutes until deep red.
  3. Pour in the boiled pepper puree, add seasonings, and simmer for 15 minutes to create the base stew (obe ata).
  4. Wash the rice thoroughly to remove starch. Stir the rice into the stew, adding the chicken broth and bay leaves.
  5. Cover tightly with foil and a lid to trap steam. Cook on very low heat for 30 minutes until rice is tender and bottom is slightly charred (the "party rice" flavor).

Swap Broth → Vegetable Stock

Confidence: High

Replace the chicken broth with a rich vegetable stock or water and vegetable bouillon.

Why? The primary flavor of Jollof comes from the roasted red peppers and tomatoes, so swapping the stock keeps the dish entirely authentic and plant-based.

Check Bouillon Cubes

Confidence: Med

Jollof rice is naturally gluten-free! Just be cautious with your choice of stock/bouillon cubes.

Why? Some commercial bouillon cubes (like certain Maggi or Knorr varieties) use wheat starch as a filler, so always check the label.

Egusi Soup

A rich, savory, and thick stew made from ground melon seeds and leafy greens.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups Ground Egusi (Melon Seeds)
  • 1/2 cup Palm Oil
  • 1 lb Assorted Meat (Beef, Tripe) & Dried Fish
  • 1 cup Chopped Spinach or Bitterleaf
  • 2 tbsp Ground Crayfish

Instructions

  1. Boil the meat with onions and bouillon until tender. Reserve the meat stock.
  2. In a small bowl, mix the ground egusi with a little water to form a thick paste.
  3. Heat the palm oil in a large pot. Add diced onions, then carefully drop spoonfuls of the egusi paste into the oil. Fry for 10 minutes until crumbly.
  4. Stir in the reserved meat stock, crayfish, and dried fish. Simmer for 15 minutes.
  5. Fold in the chopped spinach, let it wilt for 2 minutes, and serve hot with fufu or pounded yam.

Customize Recipe

Suggested Next Steps

Swap Meat/Crayfish → Tofu & Kelp Powder

Confidence: High

Replace the meat with fried firm tofu or mushrooms, and substitute the crayfish with a pinch of kelp powder or nori flakes.

Why? Ground crayfish provides a deep umami, sea-like flavor essential to the dish, which seaweed/kelp replicates beautifully.

Naturally Gluten-Free

Confidence: High

Egusi seeds, meats, and leafy greens contain zero gluten.

Why? The soup gets its thick, luxurious texture entirely from the protein-rich melon seeds, not flour.

Spicy Beef Suya

Thinly sliced, skewered beef heavily marinated in a spicy, roasted peanut spice blend and grilled to perfection.

Ingredients

  • 1.5 lbs Beef Sirloin, cut into thin strips
  • 1 cup Roasted Peanut Powder
  • 2 tbsp Cayenne Pepper & 1 tbsp Ginger Powder
  • 1 tsp Garlic Powder & 1 tsp Onion Powder
  • Vegetable Oil (for brushing)

Instructions

  1. In a bowl, mix the peanut powder, cayenne, ginger, garlic, and onion powder to create the Yaji (Suya spice) blend.
  2. Thread the thin beef strips tightly onto soaked wooden skewers.
  3. Brush the beef skewers generously with oil, then press them firmly into the dry Suya spice blend to coat entirely.
  4. Grill over hot coals or bake at 400°F for 12-15 minutes, flipping once, until the edges are charred.
  5. Serve immediately with fresh sliced red onions, tomatoes, and extra spice mix on the side.

Suggested Next Steps

Swap Beef → Seitan or Portobello Mushrooms

Confidence: High

Thread thick strips of seitan or whole portobello mushrooms onto the skewers instead of beef.

Why? The intense, nutty, spicy flavor of Suya comes entirely from the peanut coating, which adheres perfectly to dense mushrooms or plant proteins.

Naturally Gluten-Free

Confidence: High

As long as your spices are pure, this street food classic is 100% gluten-free.

Why? The crust is formed from ground peanuts rather than breading or flour.

Maafe (Groundnut Soup)

A creamy, savory, and mildly spicy peanut butter stew loaded with chicken and root vegetables.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup Unsweetened Peanut Butter
  • 1.5 lbs Chicken pieces
  • 2 Sweet Potatoes, cubed
  • 3 tbsp Tomato Paste
  • 1 large Onion, chopped
  • 4 cups Chicken Broth

Instructions

  1. Brown the chicken pieces in a large pot with a little oil, then remove and set aside.
  2. In the same pot, sauté the onions and tomato paste until the paste darkens.
  3. Whisk the peanut butter with a cup of warm chicken broth until smooth, then add to the pot along with the remaining broth.
  4. Return the chicken to the pot and bring to a simmer.
  5. Add the cubed sweet potatoes and simmer for 30 minutes until the chicken is cooked through and the oil separates to the top of the rich stew.

Suggested Next Steps

Swap Chicken → Chickpeas and Kale

Confidence: High

Omit the chicken and load the stew with canned chickpeas, extra sweet potatoes, and fresh kale.

Why? The peanut butter base is incredibly hearty. Chickpeas add great texture and protein without competing with the rich, nutty flavor.

Naturally Gluten-Free

Confidence: High

This savory peanut stew requires no modifications to be gluten-free.

Why? African stews rarely use roux (flour/butter). The thickness here comes naturally from the creamy peanut butter and starch from the sweet potatoes.

Red Red (Black-Eyed Pea Stew)

A beloved Ghanaian stew made with tender black-eyed peas, palm oil, and served with sweet fried plantains.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups Dried Black-Eyed Peas, soaked
  • 1/2 cup Red Palm Oil
  • 3 ripe Tomatoes, blended
  • 1 Onion, finely chopped
  • 2 Ripe Plantains (for frying on the side)

Instructions

  1. Boil the soaked black-eyed peas in water until very tender and soft, then drain.
  2. Heat the red palm oil in a saucepan. Sauté the onions until translucent.
  3. Add the blended tomatoes, salt, and pepper. Fry the sauce for about 15 minutes until it reduces and thickens.
  4. Gently fold the cooked black-eyed peas into the tomato stew. Simmer for 10 minutes to let the flavors marry.
  5. Slice the ripe plantains, fry them in oil until golden and caramelized, and serve alongside the stew.

Naturally Vegan

Confidence: High

This dish is entirely plant-based by design!

Why? Red Red relies on the savory depth of red palm oil and sweet tomatoes rather than meat for its rich flavor.
